Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers (SHPE) - UMD Chapter

The Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers (SHPE) is is the nation’s largest association dedicated to fostering Hispanic leadership in the STEM field. Its membership exceeds 13,000 FAMILIA members in more than 286 student and professional chapters across the United States and Puerto Rico.

SHPE granted a chapter to the University of Maryland, College Park in May 1988 to a group of 27 Hispanic students. Since then, SHPE-UMD chapter has been an organization of diverse students in engineering and science at the University of Maryland. SHPE strives to support the professional growth, the development of social responsibility, the assurance of educational opportunities for students as well as long-term achievements of Hispanic self-determination and self-actualization. SHPE-UMD’s goal is to assist students in all engineering and science fields with their academic and professional development.

President

June 2021 - May 2022

As President of the SHPE chapter at UMD, I led a 10-person executive board as our organization focused on transition back to a physical presence. Spearheaded numerous events that focused on academic and professional development, often sponsored with many of our corporate partners. Planned and executed several social events to promote the Latinx community in STEM. Started a weekly afterschool STEM program at a local majority-Hispanic elementary school. Established SHPE as a Computer Science student organization and extended our reach to all Latinx students studying Information Science and Computer Science.
